As a topological state of matter capable of realizing quantized transverse conductance without an external magnetic field, the quantum anomalous Hall effect (QAHE) holds immense potential for applications in low-power-consumption topological electronic devices. Moving beyond the traditional band theory framework based on Berry curvature integration, this paper rigorously derives the QAHE in a two-dimensional massive Dirac system strictly within the theoretical framework of pure quantum field theory. Starting from the Lagrangian of a 2+1 dimensional massive Dirac field, we first employ Noether’s theorem to derive the conserved current associated with the global U(1) gauge symmetry. Subsequently, by introducing minimal coupling to treat the external electromagnetic field as a perturbation and based on the Gell-Mann-Low theorem, we rigorously calculate the vacuum polarization tensor at the one-loop approximation. Finally, in the zero-temperature and static limits, we extract the antisymmetric part of the polarization tensor and combine it with the Kubo formula to derive an analytical expression for the quantized anomalous Hall conductance, which is determined solely by the Dirac mass term. The theoretical results not only provide a field-theoretic microscopic explanation for the half-integer conductance contribution of a single Dirac cone, but also perfectly align with cutting-edge experimental observations in magnetic topological insulator thin films, fully demonstrating the rigor and universality of quantum field theory methods in addressing many-body problems in condensed matter physics.
