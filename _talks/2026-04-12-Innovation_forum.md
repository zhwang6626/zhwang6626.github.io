Physics Tournament (PT) has been introduced to China since 2008. In China, it serves not only as a program for high school students but also for undergraduate students. As an effective opportunity for early-stage undergraduates to enhance their research training competence, PT is welcomed by many universities. However, there are also many students who dislike this type of competition. Based on our educational practices, the biggest problem lies in the perceived separation between PT and undergraduate research training. In this presentation, we trace the journey of an ordinary PT participant, illustrate the necessity of engaging in PT, and propose transforming the traditional PT into a novel Project + Teamwork model. We further demonstrate how this model bridges the gap between PT and undergraduate research training by cultivating both hard skills (e.g., literature management, experimental design, data analysis) and soft skills (e.g., critical thinking, teamwork, resilience), and by following a research workflow that naturally leads from problem identification to paper writing—thus enabling a spiral-up improvement in research competence for lower-year undergraduates.
