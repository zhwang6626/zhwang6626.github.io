In 2005, *Science* listed 125 challenging scientific questions, and the classic problem "What is the structure of water?" is one of the important ones. Although the microstructure of water has been investigated for hundreds of years, much controversy and confusion remain. Fortunately, we already know that hydrogen bonds play a significant role. As the first presentation for the Physics Seminar II course, we reviewed previous work on the essence of hydrogen bonds in liquid water, and hope to provide further insights into our subsequent research.
